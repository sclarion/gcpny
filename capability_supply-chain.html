We have a long history of improving supply chain performance in a range of manufacturing and distribution companies. Our robust analytical capabilities and ability to tackle complex situations are particularly well-suited to cross-functional and interconnected nature of supply chain operations. Whether developing a statistical inventory model to optimize inventory levels, designing cross- functional S&OP process for creating demand forecasts, or optimizing a distribution network, our capabilities cover every aspect of a supply chain, including:

Results

Turning Around Operations at Industrial Goods Manufacturer

Achieved a 40% reduction in backlog and 32% improvement in on-time delivery by first focusing on tactical fixes to get backlog situation under control and then addressing systematic supply chain issues via a including segmented operations and service strategy, new MRP parameters based on statistical models, key process redesign, and KPIs/reporting

Global Sourcing Optimization at Direct Marketing Company

Helped build strategic sourcing capabilities by migrating sourcing responsibilities from merchandising to a dedicated sourcing team, and developing detailed sourcing processes (e.g., quote management, vendor management, quality management); crafted business case for board approval of Asia Sourcing Office to enable efficient sourcing management

New Transportation and Logistics Strategy for Baked Goods Company

Created new logistics strategy, including a new DSD network and delivery process, a new transportation and warehousing strategy, and re-balanced of production among plants; over 75% of the $2.5MM in savings (from a cost base of $12MM) from the new strategy was saved in the first year of implementation

Warehouse Performance Turnaround at Household Products Company

Turned around a problematic distribution operation that was creating service problems with its leading retail customers; developed SOPs for basic warehouse functions, including inventory transactions, pick/pack/ship operation, cycle counting, and quality audits; revamped warehouse organization structure; and instituted measurement/tracking/reporting to monitor performance
